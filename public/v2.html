Canadian Banks Are Quietly Clearing Thousands of Vehicles Right Now — And Buyers With Bad Credit Are Taking Advantage

Staff Report  ·  Updated March 2025  ·  2 min read
Repo and fleet vehicles sitting at a clearing facility — banks are under pressure to move them fast.

If you've been turned down for a car loan in the past year, or if dealership prices have felt completely out of reach, there's something happening right now in Canada that most people haven't heard about yet.

Banks and fleet operators across the country are sitting on an unusual amount of vehicle inventory. Repos, off-lease returns, and rental fleet offloads are all clearing at the same time — and lenders are under pressure to move them fast.

"When lenders have too much inventory, the rules change. Approvals that normally wouldn't happen start happening. Monthly payments that should be $800 drop to $263."

This isn't a permanent situation. It's a window. And right now, that window is open.

What does this mean for someone with bad credit? It means that the same lenders who might have rejected you six months ago are now approving people with credit scores as low as 400. The math is simple — they'd rather get the vehicle off their lot at a lower monthly payment than have it sit there depreciating.
